☘ How is the weather forecast made?

Weather forecasting starts with observing the current state of the atmosphere. Knowing its current condition, forecasters can then predict upcoming weather changes in the coming days or weeks. “We live in an ocean of air, all weather changes depend on solar radiation. The state of the air ocean speaks more about future weather than about the weather at the moment, ”wrote Robert Fitzroy, founder and head of the British Meteorological Department, the future Met Office, in the introduction to his Weather Book. The first ever published weather forecast was compiled by Robert Fitzroy. It was published in the English newspaper The Times on August 1, 1861. According to one version, it was the inaccuracy of his forecasts that became the reason for his voluntary death. Numerous weather sensors on and above the Earth, at sea and in orbit measure a range of weather parameters that help paint the most complete picture of the weather on our planet. Weather information is collected by meteorological organizations around the globe, and then national weather services share it with their counterparts in other countries. The main weather parameters include: temperature, atmospheric pressure, humidity, wind speed and direction, precipitation and their amount. To measure them on land, a network of meteorological stations operates. There are 1670 such weather stations in Russia, while, for example, in China there are more than 53 thousand of them. They can be operated by meteorologists or be fully automated. In the United States, for example, there is a network of automated observation systems (ASOS) for the surface. Such weather stations are installed in more than 900 airports throughout the country, where they collect information about weather phenomena.

Weather forecasting methods

Weather prediction is considered to be the ultimate goal of atmospheric research. Forecasting is noted as the most developed area in meteorology. The nature of modern weather forecasting is quite complex. It is customary to distinguish three methods of scientific weather forecasting: synoptic weather forecasting, numerical (aka hydrodynamic) method, and statistical.
